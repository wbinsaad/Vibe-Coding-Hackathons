## Problem Statement

{{ page.problem }}

Traditional user research tools required researchers to manually aggregate data from surveys, interviews, analytics platforms, and usability tests. This fragmented workflow resulted in:
- Time-consuming data consolidation (averaging 8-12 hours per research cycle)
- Inconsistent visualization standards across team members
- Difficulty identifying patterns across multiple data sources
- Limited real-time collaboration capabilities

## Method

<div class="portfolio-accordion">
  <div class="accordion-item">
    <button class="accordion-header" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="method-content" id="method-header">
      <span class="accordion-title">Research & Design Methodology</span>
      <span class="accordion-icon" aria-hidden="true">▶</span>
    </button>
    <div class="accordion-content" id="method-content" aria-labelledby="method-header" hidden>
      <h3>Discovery Phase</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Stakeholder Interviews (n=12):</strong> Conducted contextual inquiries with UX researchers, product managers, and data analysts to understand pain points and workflows</li>
        <li><strong>Competitive Analysis:</strong> Evaluated 8 existing research platforms to identify gaps and opportunities</li>
        <li><strong>User Journey Mapping:</strong> Mapped current-state workflows to identify friction points and automation opportunities</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Design & Prototyping</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Participatory Design Sessions:</strong> 6 co-design workshops with end users to ideate dashboard layouts and interaction patterns</li>
        <li><strong>Information Architecture:</strong> Card sorting exercises (n=15 participants) to structure data hierarchy and navigation</li>
        <li><strong>Interactive Prototyping:</strong> Created high-fidelity prototypes in Figma with functional data visualization components</li>
        <li><strong>Accessibility Testing:</strong> WCAG 2.1 AA compliance testing with screen reader users and keyboard-only navigation validation</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Validation & Iteration</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Usability Testing:</strong> 3 rounds of moderated testing (n=8 per round) with task-based scenarios</li>
        <li><strong>A/B Testing:</strong> Compared visualization approaches for key metrics (card-based vs. graph-based layouts)</li>
        <li><strong>Heuristic Evaluation:</strong> Expert review against Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
